## Chapter 1: The Psychology of the Loop

The brain hates open loops. The "Zeigarnik Effect" states that people remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks. These open loops cause anxiety.

*   "Did I email John back?"
*   "What was I supposed to buy for dinner?"
*   "When is that report due?"

Every open loop is a background process consuming your RAM.

**The Solution**: A trusted system outside your brain. The Weekly Review collects all these open loops and closes them, freeing up your mental RAM for deep creative work.

## Chapter 2: The Setup (Friday vs. Sunday)

When should you plan?

### Option A: The Friday Shutdown
*   **Pros**: You leave work with a clear head. You can enjoy the weekend guilt-free.
*   **Cons**: You might be tired by Friday afternoon.

### Option B: The Sunday Reset
*   **Pros**: You are fresh. You can look at the week ahead with a calm mind.
*   **Cons**: It cuts into your leisure time.

**Recommendation**: Do the "Cleanup" on Friday and the "Strategic Planning" on Sunday.

## Chapter 3: The 5-Step Weekly Review Protocol

Follow this checklist effectively every week.

### Step 1: Clear the Decks (Capture)
*   Empty your physical inbox (mail, receipts).
*   Empty your digital inbox (get to Inbox Zero).
*   Check your Desktop/Downloads folder and file documents.
*   **Goal**: Zero friction.

### Step 2: Look Back (Reflect)
*   Review your calendar from the past week.
    *   What meetings happened? Do they require follow-up?
    *   What tasks did you not complete? (In **GoalSlot**, scan your dashboard).
*   **Journaling Question**: "What was the biggest win? What was the biggest distraction?"

### Step 3: Look Forward (Plan)
*   Review your calendar for the *next* 2 weeks.
    *   What meetings are coming up? Do you need to prep?
    *   Are there any deadlines?

### Step 4: Prioritize (Select)
*   Choose your 3 "Big Rocks" for the week. If you get nothing else done, these 3 must happen.
*   Apply the rule of Essentialism: "If it's not a definite yes, it's a no."

### Step 5: Time Block (Schedule)
*   Open **GoalSlot**.
*   Place your "Big Rocks" into the calendar first (Deep Work blocks).
*   Schedule your admin time/meetings around them.
*   **Constraint**: Leave 20% of your time empty for the unexpected.

## Chapter 4: Execution - Surviving Monday

You have a plan. Then Monday punches you in the face.

### The Daily Standup (With Yourself)
Every morning at 8:50 AM (before the world wakes up):
1.  Look at your Weekly Plan.
2.  Look at today's Time Blocks.
3.  Ask: "Is this still realistic?"
4.  Adjust.

### Dealing with "Fires"
When a crisis emerges:
1.  Don't just react.
2.  Look at your plan and ask: "What do I have to delete to handle this fire?"
3.  Consciously trade the time.

## Chapter 5: Advanced Tactics

### Theme Days
Jack Dorsey (Twitter/Square) famously used Theme Days.
*   **Monday**: Management & macro strategy.
*   **Tuesday**: Product.
*   **Wednesday**: Marketing & Growth.
*   **Thursday**: Developers & Partnerships.
*   **Friday**: Culture & Recruiting.

Even if you aren't a CEO, you can use this. Make Tuesday your "Meeting Day" and Wednesday your "Deep Work Day".

### The "No-Meeting" Wednesday
Protect one day a week at all costs. This is your "Maker Time" (as Paul Graham puts it).
